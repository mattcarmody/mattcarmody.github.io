Language Studies

In recent years, I've caught the joy of language learning. Connecting across cultures and thinking in another language have been a source of internal growth and shifted perspectives. Right now I'm working to expand my Portuguese and develop my Spanish.

Portuguese - Conversational

I've spent nearly a year in Brazil, often staying with Brazilian families. It took longer than expected and was frustrating at times, but I eventually built up to conversational Portuguese. With a beautiful sound and cadence, I love speaking Portuguese whenever I get the chance.
These days my studies center around my daily Anki reviews. Every week or two I find new Anki material by reading books or BBC articles.

Spanish - Advanced Beginner

I studied Spanish in junior high and high school but never really embraced the subject or applied what I learned. I revisited the language before my travels to Spain, Nicaragua and Argentina but the conversations I had depended on a very patient partner.
I've revived my studies, with a focus on listening comprehension. I review Anki audio cards daily and I'm exploring how best to approach learning Spanish through Portuguese.

Esperanto - Beginner

It's simple, it's logical, it's fun! I love the idea of Esperanto and it's logical construction. I started studying a while back but decided to put it on hold because I was spreading myself too thin.

Recommended Resources

Anki

I recommend this over all over resources. It is the core of my language studies. Anki is a spaced-repetition flashcard program that tracks what you know and shows you the cards at just the right time. It's the best memory tool I've used and I use it for languages, programming and miscellaneous knowledge. Anyone who's been to med school probably knows it.

Duolingo

It's a good starting point if you don't want to pay but if you're serious about learning a language I don't think it should be your main practice source. I recommend starting with it and pairing it with a spaced repetition software like Anki to retain the knowledge you pick up. Once you've gotten through the basics, if you're serious about continuing, buy Fluent Forever.

Fluent Forever (book)

A thorough guide to shape your approach to language learning. I also recommend his minimal pair trainers, Anki decks for sale that help shape your hearing and pronunciation.

Tandem

Books and apps are the training they provide is important, but the real goal is usually conversation. There is no substitute for actually talking to people. Tandem is a language exchange app to meet people who know the language you're seeking and vice versa. It's a good resource if there isn't a local scene to practice your target language.

IE Languages

A cool resource that shows similar languages side by side. For example: English, Spanish, Portuguese, French and Italian vocabulary are in a single table with toggles for each language. I haven't really used it yet, I just find it satisfying to browse.
